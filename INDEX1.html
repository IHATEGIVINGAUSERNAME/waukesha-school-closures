Waukesha District ACT Performance Analysis

Three-Year Trends: 2022-23 through 2024-25

Total Students Tested: 4,656

✓ Positive Trends

  • Grade 9 Recovery: Composite score rebounded to 16.58 (highest of 3 years)
  • Reading Improvement: Grade 9 reading up from 16.82 to 18.04
  • Math Stability: Maintaining strong performance (16.7-17.8 range)
  • Grade 9 Momentum: All subjects showing upward trajectory

✗ Concerning Trends

  • Grade 10 Decline: Composite dropped from 17.98 to 17.26
  • English Weakness: Lowest scores across all subjects (13.86-15.30)
  • Science Decline (Gr 10): Dropped from 17.77 to 16.91
  • Lost Ground: Grade 10 not maintaining Grade 9 gains

⚠ Watch Areas

  • Grade 10 Plateau: Minimal change from 23-24 to 24-25
  • STEM Scores: Solid but showing slight softness in Grade 10
  • Student Population: Slight fluctuations year-to-year
  • Subject Gaps: 3-4 point spread between strongest/weakest areas

Critical Equity Gaps Requiring Immediate Attention

Persistent achievement gaps remain the district's most significant challenge. Despite overall stable performance, substantial and consistent disparities exist across all three years of data:

Students with Disabilities score 3-4 points below district average (13.3-13.9 vs 16.1-17.3). English Learners face even larger gaps of 4-5 points below English-proficient peers (12.1-12.8 vs 16.4-18.2). Economically disadvantaged students consistently score 2-3.5 points lower than their advantaged peers.

Racial disparities are pronounced: White students score 3-4 points higher than Hispanic students and 4-5 points higher than Black students across all years. These gaps have not narrowed, indicating systemic barriers that require targeted, evidence-based interventions.

Without focused equity initiatives, these gaps will continue to limit opportunities for underserved students and undermine the district's overall mission of educational excellence for all.
